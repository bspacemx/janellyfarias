This is my story

I am a Mexican American soccer player currently playing for Chivas Femenil in the LIGA MX,among many other valuable things.
I am a woman, I am gay, I am resilient, I am loving, I am human.

CONTINUE READING

I was born on February 12, 1990 in Santa Ana, California.


I was raised by diligent, loving, Mexican born parents who came to the US seeking a better quality of life for themselves and for my brothers and me.

I pride myself in my Mexican culture. I have four amazing brothers: Francisco, Dorian, Christian, and Giovanni. My mom is my number one fan. Her name is Lupita. My dad, Francisco Sr. was one of the reasons I began playing soccer. He was my first and most impactful coach. Without him, I wouldn’t have the term “discipline” so instilled in my lifestyle. 

I was 4 years old when I first started playing soccer

My parents didn’t want me to because at that time, “soccer wasn’t for girls”. I didn’t ask them to play. I told them. That’s when my soccer career began.

I grew up playing with my brothers and boys until I was 13. I was constantly looked down upon for being a girl, but that never stopped me.

At 16 I had my first call up with the Mexico Women’s National Team. At 17 I played in my first game with the full team vs the United States. It felt like one day I was admiring them and then the next day I was playing against Christine Lilly, Heather O'Reilly, Hope Solo and alongside Maribel Dominguez (Mexico's all-time leading goal scorer amongst both the men's and women’s teams). WHOA!

In 2006, I played in the FIFA u-20 Women’s World Cup in Russia

Yeah, I was still in high school, missed 50 plus days of school a year, and still managed to graduate with a 4.0 GPA. In 2008, in a preparation match 1 week before the U-20 world cup, I blew out my left knee, all while I had my first girlfriend and was struggling with my parents and family accepting me for who I was. Physically and emotionally distraught, the struggle was just beginning.
